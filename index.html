When systolic and diastolic fall into different categories, the higher category governs. Diagnosis of hypertension (outside a crisis) is based on an average of ≥2 readings on ≥2 separate occasions, not a single chairside value.

MethodHypertension if ≥

White-coat hypertension: office ≥130/80 but out-of-office normal. Masked hypertension: office normal but out-of-office elevated — both carry real cardiovascular risk and are not "false positives" to dismiss.

Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy

CategoryDefinition
Chronic hypertension≥140/90, predates pregnancy or diagnosed before 20 weeks
Gestational hypertension≥140/90 new after 20 weeks, without proteinuria/organ dysfunction
Preeclampsia≥140/90 after 20 weeks + proteinuria or evidence of maternal organ dysfunction
Preeclampsia with severe features≥160/110, or lower BP plus severe symptoms (headache, visual changes, epigastric pain, thrombocytopenia)

A pregnant patient with a new BP ≥140/90, or any BP ≥160/110, needs same-day obstetric evaluation — treat this as more urgent than the general adult crisis threshold. Pediatric BP is assessed against age/sex/height-specific percentile charts, not fixed adult cut-offs.

Hypertensive Crisis — Action

◆ Hypertensive urgency

BP ≥180 and/or ≥120 without symptoms. Have the patient rest quietly 5 minutes, recheck. If it remains elevated, defer elective/invasive dental care and arrange same-day medical evaluation — no EMS call needed if truly asymptomatic.

✖ Hypertensive emergency

Same BP range with symptoms of target-organ damage. STOP all treatment, keep the patient seated and calm, call emergency services immediately. Do not give further local anesthetic with vasoconstrictor.

Low BP / Syncope — Action

△ Orthostatic hypotension

Reposition the chair back upright in stages rather than all at once; let the patient sit for 1–2 minutes before standing. Common with diuretics, alpha-blockers, and in the elderly.

✓ Vasovagal reaction

Recline the chair (legs slightly elevated), loosen tight clothing, ensure airway, apply a cool cloth. Most resolve within 1–2 minutes; recheck vitals before resuming.

⚠ Local Anesthetic & Vasoconstrictor Caution

Well-controlled hypertension: standard epinephrine-containing cartridges (1:100,000 or 1:200,000) are generally safe.
Uncontrolled/severe hypertension (≥180/110): avoid elective use of vasoconstrictor-containing LA until BP is controlled.
Non-selective beta-blockers: limit dose, aspirate before injecting, avoid epinephrine-containing retraction cord.
Always: use the minimum effective dose and aspirate before every injection.

NSAID Interaction Warning

NSAIDs can raise blood pressure and blunt the effect of diuretics, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, and beta-blockers. Avoid prolonged NSAID courses in poorly controlled hypertensives — acetaminophen is a reasonable alternative for dental pain when appropriate.

Never Forget
  • Never advise stopping antihypertensive medication without physician guidance
  • Never treat electively at BP ≥180/110
  • Treat the patient, not just the number — recheck before deciding
  • Recheck BP after any hypotensive episode before resuming
  • If in doubt, postpone elective treatment

Dental Practice Considerations

SituationGuidance
Appointment timingMorning appointments are generally less stressful and reduce cumulative anxiety-driven BP elevation over the day.
Baseline BPRecord BP at the first visit for every adult patient and at recall visits for known hypertensives — untreated hypertension is often asymptomatic and dental visits can be an early detection point.
Before sedation/GA or surgical proceduresCheck BP immediately before the procedure regardless of history.
Stress reduction protocolShort waiting times, clear communication, adequate anesthesia before starting, consider nitrous oxide/oxygen for anxious patients, and effective post-op pain control.
Chair repositioningRaise/lower the chair slowly and in stages, particularly for elderly patients or those on diuretics/alpha-blockers, to prevent orthostatic hypotension.
Recheck protocolA single elevated chairside reading is not a diagnosis — let the patient rest quietly 5 minutes and recheck before deciding to defer treatment.

  • Before surgical procedures
  • Before LA with vasoconstrictor in cardiac patients
  • Symptoms of dizziness/headache
  • Pregnant patients, every visit
  • Recurrent recall visits

MAP = DBP + ⅓ (SBP − DBP); normal MAP is roughly 70–100 mmHg. Pulse pressure >60 mmHg in an older adult is associated with arterial stiffness and higher cardiovascular risk.

Special Situations

Pregnancy

New BP ≥140/90 or any ≥160/110 needs same-day obstetric evaluation, not routine follow-up.

Elderly

Higher orthostatic hypotension risk; isolated systolic hypertension is common — reposition chair slowly.

Renal disease

Both a cause and a consequence of hypertension; NSAID use needs extra caution.

Obesity / OSA

Common hypertension comorbidity; may need a larger cuff for an accurate reading and careful positioning.

Post-MI / stroke

Avoid elective dental treatment within the first ~6 months without cardiology/neurology clearance.

Pacemaker / ICD

Common in cardiac patients on antihypertensives — avoid electrocautery near the device; standard LA is safe.

Diabetes comorbidity

Hypertension and diabetes commonly coexist and compound cardiovascular and healing risk together.

Anxiety / white coat

Can transiently raise office BP — recheck after the patient settles before acting on a single high reading.

Measurement Precautions

  • Correct cuff size for arm circumference
  • Patient seated, back supported, feet flat
  • Arm at heart level
  • No talking during measurement
  • No caffeine/exercise in prior 30 min
  • Let patient rest ≥5 min before reading
  • Recheck before acting on any high reading
  • Use the same arm for serial comparisons
